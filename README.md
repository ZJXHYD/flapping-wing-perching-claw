## Project Overview

This project presents a mechanically coupled **perching claw mechanism** designed for a micro flapping-wing aerial robot. The goal of this mechanism is to enable the robot to **perch on branches or structures after flight**, similar to how birds grasp surfaces.

The system integrates a **compact gear transmission** and a **linkage-driven claw mechanism** to coordinate the motion of multiple claws using a single actuation input.

---

## Design Concept

The mechanism is designed to achieve three main objectives:

• Lightweight structure suitable for micro aerial vehicles  
• Synchronized motion of multiple claw segments  
• Efficient mechanical transmission using minimal actuators  

A **gear train distributes motion** from the input shaft through several spur gears, which drive the linkage system connected to the claw segments.

---

## Mechanical Structure

The system consists of three main subsystems:

### 1. Gear Transmission
A series of spur gears distributes the actuation torque and synchronizes the motion of the mechanism.

### 2. Linkage Mechanism
Mechanical linkages transmit motion from the gear system to the distal claw joints.

### 3. Claw Segments
Multiple articulated claw segments close simultaneously to grasp cylindrical objects such as branches or rods.

---

## CAD Design and Prototyping

The mechanism was designed using **SolidWorks**.  
After completing the CAD design, a prototype was fabricated using **3D printing** to verify the feasibility of the mechanical transmission and linkage synchronization.
